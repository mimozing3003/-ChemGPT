Explain the laboratory synthesis of Vanillin from guaiacol and provide the reaction mechanism.

terminal
Vanillin (4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzaldehyde) can be synthesized through several industrial and laboratory routes. One common laboratory method is the Reimer–Tiemann reaction, though industrially, the glyoxylic acid route from guaiacol is preferred for higher yields.
function Molecular Definition
C_8H_8O_3 \quad \text{(Vanillin)}

Mechanism Overview:

1. ACTIVATION: Guaiacol reacts with glyoxylic acid in an aqueous alkaline medium.

2. CONDENSATION: An electrophilic aromatic substitution occurs at the para-position relative to the hydroxyl group.
